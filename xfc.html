Problem

Coordinated control in real time.

The controller needed to navigate, orient, engage threats, and stay consistent across changing scenarios.

The Kessler Game demands fast decisions across coupled control tasks. Good movement alone is not enough. The controller needs coordinated outputs, stable learning, and behavior that generalizes beyond one setup.

  • Navigation pressureMovement and rotation decisions affect survivability and positioning.
  • Combat pressureFire and mine actions have to align with threat timing and intent.
  • Generalization pressureThe controller needs scenario awareness instead of brittle one-off behavior.
Approach

Hybrid control for stability and speed.

System Design

Input State

Scenario context, relative velocity, TTC, and spatial features.

Fuzzy Policy

Sugeno-based maneuver policy for thrust and turn decisions.

Scenario Bias

Scenario-conditioned offsets adapt the policy across encounter types.

Warm Start

Behavior-cloning initialization improved early stability.

PPO Loop

GAE and KL early stopping improved training efficiency.

Hybrid Control

Learned maneuvering stayed paired with structured combat logic.

Why this design

  • Warm-started policyStarted PPO from a pretrained maneuver controller instead of a cold start.
  • Scenario-conditioned policyAdded small scenario-specific biases without discarding the fuzzy base model.
  • Hybrid control splitKept learned maneuvering and structured combat logic in a practical setup.
Training Pipeline

How the controller was trained.

1. Warm start

Initialized PPO from the pretrained maneuver bundle instead of training from scratch.

2. Foundation stage

Started with readable scenarios like stock, donut ring, and vertical wall to stabilize core movement behavior.

3. Motion and pressure stages

Expanded into harder scenario groups to train crossing behavior, denser asteroid motion, and pressure handling.

4. PPO updates with scenario context

Used GAE, mini-batch PPO, KL-based early stopping, and context inputs during the full run.
