Problem

Coordinated control in real time.

The controller needed to navigate, orient, engage threats, and stay consistent across changing scenarios.

The Kessler Game demands fast decisions across coupled control tasks. Good movement alone is not enough. The controller needs coordinated outputs, stable learning, and behavior that generalizes beyond one setup.

  • Navigation pressureMovement and rotation decisions affect survivability and positioning.
  • Combat pressureFire and mine actions have to align with threat timing and intent.
  • Generalization pressureThe controller needs scenario awareness instead of brittle one-off behavior.
Approach

Hybrid control for stability and speed.

The hybrid design gave the policy a stronger starting point and better sample efficiency than a blank-slate PPO setup.

Why this design

  • Shared-trunk policyOne learned representation supported all control outputs.
  • Behavior-cloning warm startImproved the initial policy and early training stability.
  • Hybrid control logicBalanced learned adaptation with structured heuristics.
Training Pipeline

Training pipeline.

The training-time reduction came from several decisions working together.

1. Generalized Advantage Estimation

Used GAE to stabilize updates and improve the bias-variance tradeoff.

2. Mini-batch PPO updates

Structured the PPO loop for more efficient training and steadier learning.

3. KL-divergence early stopping

Limited aggressive policy drift and improved training stability.

4. Scenario-conditioned signals

Used time-to-collision, relative velocity, and proximity to improve prioritization.
